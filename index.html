FAQs

The cheetah is the fastest land animal, capable of reaching speeds up to 70 mph (113 km/h) in short bursts covering distances up to 500 meters.

Elephants typically live for 50 to 70 years in the wild, with some individuals reaching up to 80 years or more.

Penguins waddle because their legs are short and positioned far back on their bodies, making this movement energy-efficient for their anatomy.

No, not all snakes are venomous. Only about 20% of snake species are venomous, and they use venom primarily to capture prey.
